Part II: Advanced dplyr

Using the hadcrut dataset and what we know about what the anomaly column means, let's calculate the proportion of months in each decade that had temperature anomalies above or below the average. This analysis will help illustrate long-term patterns and potential evidence of climate change.

For our data set's purposes, we will have all decades starting from the year 1850 until now. Let's first identify that our data set is good in that every decade has 120 months (10 years * 12 months per year). To do this, we created a new decade column as shown below:

In[10]:
hadcrut <- hadcrut |> 
    mutate(decade = year - year %% 10)
  1. Inspect the equation in the mutate statement above closely. What does %% mean in the context of the expression above? Write a brief explanation about what this equation does.

The %% expression takes the remainder of the year variable when divided by 10. For example, if our year was 1856, then 1856 / 10 would be 185 with a remainder of 6, which is what this returns. More simply put, it is returning whatever is in the ones digit. So for this expression, we are creating a decade column by subtracting the ones digit of a given year from that year leaving us with years that correspond only to decades.
